The Series

Four ongoing bodies of work.

01Portraits

Capture You

Honest portraits — candid and styled — of the people in front of the lens. The work centers presence over performance: a quiet jaw, an unguarded laugh, the way light finds a cheekbone at golden hour.

02Landscape

Capturecations

A travel log in frames. Gothic spires, neon-soaked streets, the harbor at dusk, eucalyptus-scrubbed coastlines — places appreciated slowly, then pressed into a single still.

03Events

Capture,Vibe!

The pulse of the room — concerts, galas, prom nights, church gatherings. Movement, sweat, confetti and stage-light. Commemorating energy as it happens, not after it cools.

04Volunteering

CaptureKind

Photography in service of organizations doing good work. Women's workshops, community days, school programs — documenting the people who quietly build kinder rooms.

About

A Fujifilm XT10, three years of practice, and a soft spot for the in-between moments.

Scarlett Raine is a Hong Kong–based photographer working across events, landscape and portrait. Her practice moves between service work for volunteer organizations, travel-led location studies, and quiet portraiture.

What ties it together is restraint — a preference for honest light, generous negative space, and the breath before the picture is taken.
